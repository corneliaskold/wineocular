White Grapes

Chardonnay

The grape is relatively neutral, with fresh apple aromas. Oak ageing adds vanilla, toast or toffee aromas, together with other techniques adds layers of flavour and complexity. Chardonnay pairs best with mild, subtle foods that are not too spicy, not too pungent and not too acidic.

Sauvignon Blanc

In general, sauvignon blanc is used to make dry wines, distinctive for their fresh acidity and beautiful perfume. In cooler climates the wines often have bright citrus and mineral notes combined with a green nettle character. Cheese, green vegetables, oysters and pasta all pair well.

Pinot Grigio

This grape is charmingly light and fresh with citrus, peach and floral aromas. It's brilliant drunk on its own as an aperitif or with salads and creamy cheeses. Everyday pinot grigios are a great place to start for simple, fresh, fruity white wines for easy drinking.

Gewürztraminer

Scents of sweet spice, plump pink roses, lychees, ginger and hints of grapefruit and white peach. Gewürz is often the answer when it comes to hard-to-match spicy dishes like Thai food, Indian, Vietnamese, or Chinese dishes, anything with fragrance and a mild-medium chilli hit.

Chenin Blanc

The aromas and flavor notes include the descriptors of minerally, greengage, angelica and honey. If produced from noble rot, notes of peaches and honey that develop into barley sugar, marzipan, and quince as they age. Pairs well with Seafood,fruit desserts and tasty cheeses.

Riesling

This aromatic grape variety makes some of the world's finest white wines in a vast variety of styles, the best of which will age brilliantly, turning from floral, lime aromas and flavours to intriguing developed aromas of kerosene. Seafood, vegetables, fruit and tasty cheeses all pairs well.+
